Astronomaly:Protege applied to MGCLS

Astronomaly is a machine learning framework for anomaly detection with active learning. In Lochner & Rudnick (2024), we introduced a new method called Protege, which acts as a recommendation engine for finding interesting sources. What follows is the entire MGCLS dataset from the paper, 6161 sources, ordered from most to least interesting, according to Protege. Please use the page numbers to navigate between sets of sources. Each cutout is resized to the smallest bounding box around the source, with a bar in the bottom left indicating the beam size (roughly 7.6 arcseconds). The sources are rescaled using asinh and truncated at 90% maximum flux. The original MGCLS image and coordinates of the source in degrees are listed. The score from author ML, from 1 to 5, is indicated, with -1 meaning this source was never given a score. Sources from the evaluation subset are indicated.
